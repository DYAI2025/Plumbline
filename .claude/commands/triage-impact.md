## Context
Plumbline's prioritization lens is **authenticity**: how much an item converts a CLAIM into
ENFORCED REALITY or HONEST DISCLOSURE. The backlog lives in `dev-plan.md` and `docs/plans/*`.
The user repeatedly asks for the highest-impact next step that is *also* safe and fast — i.e.
impact-per-(safe × fast), not raw impact.

## Your Task
Read the real backlog, score candidates on impact × safety × speed, and recommend — grounded,
not from memory.

### Steps
1. **Read the actual backlog first.** `dev-plan.md`, `docs/plans/*-roadmap.md`, and any open
   plan docs. Enumerate the remaining (not-yet-shipped-to-`main`) items.
2. **Score each** on three axes: authenticity-impact, safety (blast radius), speed (bounded?).
   Optimize **impact-per-(safe × fast)** — heavy/high-impact items are penalized if they are
   not also safe and fast.
3. **For a close call, triangulate.** Fan out an independent judge panel (diverse lenses) plus
   an adversarial refutation of the front-runner. Panel agents must be **read-only** (no
   Write/Edit) — a stray tracked file reddens `run_all`.
4. **Recommend ONE move** with: the single smallest `run_all`-green first increment, an honest
   token/effort note, what it unblocks, and a named runner-up.

### Guardrails
- **Read the backlog files — never recommend from memory.**
- Penalize behavior reversals, test churn on governance paths, and unverified premises; reward
  read-only/additive work.
- **Be honest about magnitude** — don't oversell "significant" savings; cite the evidence and
  state when an effect is modest.
- Don't fully remove/rewrite when an opt-in or additive path delivers the same user benefit at
  lower risk; flag the cheaper-and-reversible option.
