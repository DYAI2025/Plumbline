# Raw idea — "stop drowning people in notifications"

This is the unpolished idea that was fed into the Product Canvas alongside `CANVAS.md`.
It is the raw, founder-voice framing the challenge council also sees. Seeded fixture for
the challenge-gate oracle measurement — neutral by construction (see RUNBOOK.md).

---

People on busy accounts are getting hammered by notifications. Every little thing that
happens on something they follow turns into its own email and its own in-app ping. On a
loud week that is a constant stream of interruptions, and we keep hearing the same thing
from accounts: there are too many, they come too often, and some users just turn the
whole thing off — which is worse for everyone, because then they genuinely miss things.

The idea: let a user say "don't send me each of these one at a time — gather up what
happened and send it to me together on a schedule I pick." So instead of forty separate
pings about one busy project, they get one grouped roll-up at, say, 8am their time, with
everything organized by the thing it relates to, and each line links straight to the
item so they can jump in if they want.

Obviously some things can't wait — if it's a security alert or an approval with a
deadline attached, that still needs to go out right away. So those categories skip the
roll-up and behave like they do today.

It should be something the individual user turns on for themselves, picks daily or
weekly, picks a time, and that's it. We are not trying to build a smart ranking engine
or let admins force a policy on a whole team in this first version. Just: gather, group,
deliver on a schedule the user controls, keep the urgent stuff immediate.

The thing I am unsure about: whether people will actually trust a delayed roll-up enough
to leave notifications on, or whether they will treat the roll-up itself as one more
thing to ignore. And whether "gather everything that happened in a window and send it
later" is going to be a nightmare to get right when people follow a huge number of items
across a lot of timezones.
